Question 4 of 5

Four people respond in the comments section of an online magazine article about extreme sport. Read the texts and then answer the questions below.

Person A:
I don't deny that these adventurous activities look exciting, but many people underestimate how demanding they are. You can't simply decide to try rock climbing or diving one day and expect to be safe. It takes time to learn from experienced instructors, to practise regularly, and to get used to the special equipment. I've seen beginners rush in without enough preparation, and the results can be dangerous. To me, gaining the right skills is the only way to enjoy such activities without unnecessary risks. I admire those who are patient enough to build their ability step by step.
Person B:
When I was younger, I spent a lot of time playing basketball and volleyball with friends. I really enjoyed the teamwork, the energy, and the feeling of competition. Later, someone introduced me to skateboarding, and at first I wasn't sure. But the more I practised, the more I felt excited by the challenge. Gradually, I began to prefer these new activities over the traditional games I used to love. Now I can't imagine my weekends without some kind of daring adventure. It gives me a sense of progress and freedom that I never found on the football field.
Person C:
My classmates often try to convince me to join them in activities like paragliding or bungee jumping, but I always refuse. I simply don't see what real benefit they bring. For me, they are unnecessary risks rather than valuable experiences. I would rather stay away from such dangers and spend my time on calmer hobbies like reading or cycling. Even though others say I'm missing out, I don't think I am. Choosing to avoid them completely makes me feel safer and more comfortable.
Person D:
For me, extreme sports are more than just excitement — they are a way to connect with nature. When I first tried kayaking in the mountains, I was surprised by how peaceful it felt, even though the river was strong. The water was cold, the air was clean, and the trees along the banks made me forget the noise of the city. If I had the chance, I would definitely do it again. Unfortunately, I don't get many opportunities nowadays, but I really wish I could enjoy those moments once more.

Question 5 of 5

Read the passage quickly. Choose a heading for each numbered paragraph (1–7) from the drop-down box. There is one more heading than you need.

Digital transformation

List of Headings:
A. Building inclusive digital ecosystems
B. Concerns about cybersecurity risks
C. Enhancing productivity through automation
D. Focusing on ethical technology development
E. Over-reliance on automated systems
F. Promoting digital skills training
G. Redefining business models with technology
H. The high costs of hardware replacement (Extra heading)

1. Digital transformation has completely reshaped how businesses define their models and strategies. Instead of relying only on physical stores and traditional advertising, companies now integrate online platforms, mobile applications, and social media channels to reach customers. Data analytics also enables firms to personalize products and services, giving customers exactly what they need at the right time. New revenue streams such as e-commerce, subscription-based services, and sharing economy platforms demonstrate how technology is rewriting the rules of competition.

2. Automation, supported by artificial intelligence, robotics, and advanced software, has allowed organizations to improve productivity on a massive scale. Factories can now operate with robotic arms that work around the clock, while offices benefit from digital assistants that handle scheduling, data entry, or even customer inquiries. This not only reduces human error but also allows employees to spend more time on creative and strategic work. For many industries, automation has become the key to staying competitive in a fast-moving global market.

3. As businesses evolve, workers must evolve with them. Digital skills such as coding, big data analysis, cybersecurity awareness, and online collaboration are becoming essential for success. Governments, schools, and private companies are investing heavily in training programs and lifelong learning opportunities. For example, many firms encourage employees to join online courses or participate in workshops on digital literacy. By doing so, societies can ensure that workers are not left behind but instead empowered to thrive in a technology-driven environment.

4. Despite the clear benefits of automation, there are risks when organizations depend too heavily on machines and algorithms. A single system failure can disrupt entire supply chains, delay services, or even put safety at risk. Moreover, relying purely on automated decision-making might neglect human judgment, empathy, and ethical considerations. For instance, an algorithm may reject a loan application unfairly because it lacks the human ability to understand personal circumstances. Striking a balance between human oversight and automation remains a critical challenge.

5. As organizations collect and store vast amounts of digital data, the threat of cyberattacks becomes more serious. Hackers constantly search for weaknesses to steal information, commit fraud, or disrupt services. High-profile incidents of data breaches have shown that even global corporations and governments are vulnerable. To protect sensitive information, businesses must invest in advanced security solutions such as firewalls, encryption, and multi-factor authentication. At the same time, educating users about safe online behavior is equally important in minimizing risks.

6. Digital transformation should not only benefit wealthy nations or large companies but also create opportunities for small businesses, rural communities, and disadvantaged groups. Many governments and organizations are now working on bridging the digital divide by improving internet access, lowering the cost of devices, and offering training programs to underrepresented populations. A truly inclusive digital ecosystem helps ensure that everyone can participate in the digital economy, fostering innovation and shared prosperity.

7. As digital technologies continue to expand, ethical concerns become increasingly important. Issues such as personal data privacy, artificial intelligence bias, and responsible use of algorithms must be addressed to build public trust. Companies and governments are encouraged to follow principles of transparency, accountability, and fairness in designing new technologies. By focusing on ethical development, societies can make sure that digital transformation not only drives economic growth but also protects human dignity and well-being for future generations.
